## Explanation

The `observation_perturb_linf` field records the linf-norm of the perturbation
**applied to the observation**, measured in pixel space (typically 0-255 range).
The actual perturbation is:

1. Generated by PGD with the specified objective and epsilon budget
2. Clamped to [-epsilon, +epsilon] in normalized space
3. Rescaled to pixel space for application and measurement

The linf=2.1191 value is the **post-clip, post-rescale measurement** in pixel
space. It reflects the PGD convergence ceiling given:
- The model's gradient landscape for the specific observation
- The epsilon budget (0.10 for random/VIS_current, 0.25 for targeted)
- The PGD step count and step size

For random and VIS_current (eps=0.10), linf=2.1191 represents the budget-saturated
perturbation. The PGD converges to the epsilon boundary.

For targeted (eps=0.25), linf=2.1191 is LOWER than the 0.25 budget would allow.
This is because `force_gripper_open_token_ce` targets specific gripper-open
token positions — the gradient focuses perturbation energy on semantically
meaningful pixels, and the linf ceiling is determined by PGD convergence rather
than the epsilon budget.

distinguishing factor, not the perturbation magnitude.
